# Category Card Art Direction

This document governs the ten category-cover cards only. Product-family cards are handled category by category after this set is approved. Product truth takes precedence over visual novelty; see `category-card-truth-sheet.md`.

## Shared invariant prompt

Use case: photorealistic natural editorial product photography.

Create a 3:4 portrait category-card photograph for the A3 Food & Beverage website. Use believable food texture, restrained styling and soft directional daylight. The image must remain immediately legible at small card size.

The upper copy zone, approximately x 5-95% and y 4-28%, must remain calm and empty through composition, not through a separate colour block. A single mostly neutral soft-ivory physical surface continues naturally from the top edge into the product scene. There must be no horizon, wall-table junction, straight tonal boundary, paper patch, gradient, fog or fade at the copy-zone limit. Nothing may enter the zone: no product, vessel, crop, leaf, prop, shadow or secondary-surface edge. Keep the upper-right arrow area equally quiet.

The product scene may begin immediately below y 28%. This percentage is an invisible object-exclusion boundary, never a visible design boundary. Move the product high enough that the card does not feel half-empty, but keep every object below the exclusion line. Use one dominant hero form occupying roughly 50-65% of the product scene and no more than two clearly subordinate supporting forms. Do not make a catalogue of equal-sized objects.

Bright and clean applies to exposure and the copy zone, not to every object. Preserve true product colours. Create recognition and separation with at least two category-authentic devices: a contrasting surface, a vessel with a different value, a true raw-material accent, translucency, backlight, or warm/cool material contrast. Ground the product grouping with one restrained secondary surface layer such as natural linen, kraft paper, a cutting board, burlap or a category-appropriate cloth. It enters organically from a side or bottom edge with a diagonal, curved or irregular silhouette; it must never create a full-width horizontal cut. Its edge, fold and contact shadow should connect the objects without becoming another backdrop, and it must remain entirely below the copy-safe boundary. The lower scene may use rich natural brown, green, red, amber, charcoal, muted blue-grey or metal where appropriate.

Do not harmonise all products to the background colour. Do not turn pale products, vessels, cloth and surface into one beige-white mass. No artificial text, labels, branding, packaging graphics, hands, people or watermark. No dark vignette, black background, night lighting, theatrical spotlight, excessive props, duplicated product forms or biologically implausible ingredients.

## Category composition matrix

| Category | Hero form | Supporting forms | Distinctive lower-scene language |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Sugar | Refined white granulated sugar | White sugar cubes; brown/raw cane crystals | Brushed steel, pale stone and unbleached natural linen; green cane and a real white sugar beet below the copy zone |
| Coffee | Green Arabica/Conilon beans | Ripe red coffee cherries; dark green leaves | Dark worn coffee wood and restrained burlap; no roasted beans |
| Cocoa Products | One opened ripe cocoa pod with fermented/dried beans | Cocoa powder; pale golden cocoa butter or dark cocoa mass | Pale stone, terracotta and dark cocoa contrast; no five-item sampler |
| Grains & Seeds | Wheat/durum grain and ears | Yellow maize; striped in-shell sunflower seed | Galvanised metal sample vessel and warm harvest textures; crops remain visibly distinct |
| Dairy & Milk Powders | Commercial milk powder | Butter; cheese or an unbranded aseptic UHT pack | Clean warm stone with restrained stainless/blue textile accents; not a fresh-milk lifestyle scene |
| Fats & Oils | Amber liquid vegetable oil | Semi-solid palm/margarine or bakery-fat slab; one true crop accent | Clear glass, warm limestone and deep green grounding; no dairy-butter curl |
| Starches & Sweeteners | Fine neutral starch powder | Amber syrup; white crystalline sweetener | Technical sample vessels with strong material separation; no green pea-protein powder |
| Dried Fruit & Nuts | Dried apricots, dates/raisins in one dominant dark vessel | Dried figs; in-shell pistachio/kernel or walnuts | Warm oak and textile with genuine fruit/nut colour; fruit must read as dried |
| Frozen Foods | Frozen potato and vegetables in one compartment | Frozen seafood/protein and pastry in separate sealed/divided areas | Frost, stainless and cold-chain order without a blue cast; never mix exposed raw poultry with produce |
| Consumer Foods | Unbranded ketchup/mayonnaise foodservice or retail forms | Dry pasta; tomato paste or a plain canned/jarred item | Warm stone with deep red, cream and durum-gold contrast; no fresh meal-prep bowl composition |

## Adjacency rule

In the ten-card overview, adjacent cards must differ in at least three of these fields: product geometry, camera angle, composition direction, lower-scene grounding value, vessel type, raw-material accent and lighting direction. The warm-paper copy zone remains identical across all cards; diversity happens below it.

Reject any image that falls back to the recurring pale bowl + pale linen + pale stone formula, even when the individual ingredients are factually correct.
