# Category Card Product Truth Sheet

This sheet is the factual gate for the ten overview images. A prompt or generated image that conflicts with it is rejected before visual review.

## Global truth rules

- Represent the category with one dominant commercial form and at most two supporting groups.
- Do not repeat the same physical form merely in different vessels.
- Raw-material accents must be the actual botanical or animal source, at a plausible maturity and colour.
- Preserve the natural colour of every product. Background colour must not tint the products.
- When a category contains many products, select distinct physical families rather than attempting to show every SKU.
- Packaging may be plain and unbranded, but it must match the commercial form: for example UHT milk belongs in an aseptic format, while canned foods should read as shelf-stable rather than fresh meal preparation.

## 1. Sugar

**Portfolio truth:** refined and less-refined cane sugar plus white beet sugar.

**Cover selection:** white granulated sugar as hero; clearly formed white sugar cubes and brown/raw cane crystals as supports. Add green cane and one real sugar beet only as lower-zone source accents.

**Colour/form controls:** a sugar beet has a pale cream/tan exterior and white fleshy taproot. Brown/raw cane sugar is golden to brown because of its molasses content. Cubes must be visibly geometric, not a second bowl of granulated sugar.

**Reject:** red table beet; two bowls of identical white granulated sugar; brown sugar presented as beet sugar; cane or beet entering the copy zone.

## 2. Coffee

**Portfolio truth:** Brazilian green Arabica and Robusta/Conilon profiles, including specialty and commercial lots.

**Cover selection:** green coffee beans as hero; ripe deep-red coffee cherries and dark green leaves as origin accents. A dark worn wooden sample surface can provide contrast.

**Colour/form controls:** commercial green coffee should read pale green/olive, not roasted brown. Red cherries signal ripe coffee fruit; green cherries may be used only when intentionally showing an unripe stage.

**Reject:** roasted beans; a finished cup of coffee; white-on-white bean, bowl and surface treatment; cherries that resemble grapes.

## 3. Cocoa Products

**Portfolio truth:** fermented/dried cocoa beans, natural or alkalised cocoa powder, cocoa butter and cocoa mass/liquor.

**Cover selection:** one opened ripe cocoa pod and dried beans as hero; choose only two processed cues, preferably powder plus either pale golden/ivory butter or dark mass.

**Colour/form controls:** fresh seeds inside an opened pod may retain pale pulp, while commercial beans must read fermented and dried brown. Cocoa butter is pale golden/ivory; cocoa mass is deep brown; powder is fine brown material.

**Reject:** five equal bowls; cocoa butter rendered like dairy butter; every product rendered the same brown; a pod crossing the copy.

## 4. Grains & Seeds

**Portfolio truth:** milling wheat, durum wheat for pasta, maize and sunflower seed.

**Cover selection:** wheat/durum grain and ears as hero; yellow maize kernels and striped in-shell sunflower seeds in distinctly different sample areas.

**Colour/form controls:** bread/milling wheat and durum must not become identical generic grain piles; durum may read harder, amber and more vitreous. Maize is a separate yellow kernel form. Sunflower seed must read as in-shell seed, not an already-refined oil cue.

**Reject:** undifferentiated beige grain assortment; all crops mixed into one bin; decorative wheat covering text.

## 5. Dairy & Milk Powders

**Portfolio truth:** whole, skimmed, instant and fat-filled powders; whey, lactose and permeate powders; butter, cheese, cheese analogues and UHT milk.

**Cover selection:** milk powder as hero; butter plus cheese or one plain aseptic UHT format as supports. The three forms must remain visually separate.

**Colour/form controls:** lactose may appear crystalline, whereas milk/whey/permeate forms are powders. Fat-filled milk powder is a formulated dairy-solids/vegetable-fat product and must not be implied to be ordinary whole milk powder. UHT should read shelf-stable, not as a fresh-milk glass-only scene.

**Reject:** cheese occupying the copy zone; a generic breakfast still life; multiple indistinguishable white powders; branded packaging.

## 6. Fats & Oils

**Portfolio truth:** sunflower, maize, olive, palm oil/palm olein, margarines, bakery fats and specialty fats.

**Cover selection:** amber liquid vegetable oil as hero; one visibly semi-solid margarine/palm/bakery-fat form and one authentic crop cue such as sunflower seed, maize, olive or palm fruit.

**Colour/form controls:** palm olein is a liquid palm fraction; palm oil and formulated fats may be semi-solid depending on the grade and temperature. A semi-solid support should read as margarine/shortening or functional fat, not dairy butter.

**Reject:** butter curl; an oil pour entering the title; too many unrelated origin crops; all-yellow monochrome treatment.

## 7. Starches & Sweeteners

**Portfolio truth:** botanical starches; wheat and pea proteins; glucose/fructose syrups, maltodextrin and dextrose; polyols; allulose and stevia.

**Cover selection:** one neutral fine starch powder as hero; amber syrup and a white crystalline sweetener as distinct supports. A small maize, cassava/potato or stevia accent may identify origin without becoming another equal group.

**Colour/form controls:** commercial pea-protein powder is generally cream/beige rather than bright green. Glycerol is a clear viscous liquid. Dextrose/polyols/allulose should read as credible crystals or powder, not glass cubes or confectionery.

**Reject:** overhead grid of many equal bowls; green pea-protein powder; a black bowl intruding behind the heading; clear gummy-like cubes.

## 8. Dried Fruit & Nuts

**Portfolio truth:** raisins/sultanas, dried apricots, dates, dried figs, in-shell pistachios, pistachio kernels and walnuts.

**Cover selection:** one dominant dried-fruit and nut assortment in a dark natural vessel; use a few separated dried figs and both an opened in-shell pistachio and kernels when pistachio is shown.

**Colour/form controls:** apricots may be sulphured orange or naturally dried brown; raisins/dates are deep amber to burgundy/brown; dried figs must visibly read wrinkled/dried, not fresh fruit; pistachio kernels provide a controlled green accent.

**Reject:** fresh juicy fig styling; neon-orange fruit; only shelled kernels standing in for the whole pistachio family; excessive equal piles.

## 9. Frozen Foods

**Portfolio truth:** frozen potato products, fruit and vegetables, poultry, seafood, and frozen pastry/bakery.

**Cover selection:** frozen potato plus vegetables as hero; frozen seafood/protein and pastry may appear only in physically separated, divided or individually contained areas.

**Colour/form controls:** frost should communicate quick-frozen condition without making the whole image blue. Raw animal protein must remain isolated from produce and bakery products. The arrangement should suggest cold-chain samples or commercial assortments, not one prepared meal.

**Reject:** exposed raw poultry touching produce; shrimp, pastry, berries and fries mixed on one open tray; meal-prep aesthetic; heavy blue cast.

## 10. Consumer Foods

**Portfolio truth:** ketchup, mayonnaise, canned foods, dry pasta and tomato paste for branded, foodservice and private-label routes.

**Cover selection:** unbranded ketchup and mayonnaise containers or serving forms as hero; dry durum pasta and one clearly shelf-stable tomato-paste or canned/jarred form as supports.

**Colour/form controls:** ketchup is a tomato-based sauce; mayonnaise is a pale emulsion; tomato paste must appear darker and more concentrated than ketchup; pasta is dry and durum-gold. A plain can or jar may be opened enough to identify the contents without labels.

**Reject:** fresh diced vegetables as the main subject; meal-prep cups; tomato paste and ketchup rendered indistinguishably; branded retail packs.
