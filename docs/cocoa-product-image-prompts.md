# Cocoa product-card image prompt set

The four cards were generated as separate images with the same visual family. Each output was prepared as an 800 × 1200 WebP for the existing `category-overlay` card system.

## Shared art direction

Editorial commercial ingredient photography, 2:3 portrait composition. Soft ivory to pale-stone seamless background with the upper 45–50% kept calm and visually clean for a dark text overlay. Arrange the ingredient scene in the lower half with physically accurate natural material, restrained food-laboratory or trade props, subtle tactile detail and soft directional daylight from the upper left. Refined, realistic, premium B2B sourcing aesthetic; muted cocoa, stone and warm-neutral palette; consistent camera height and scale across the set. No text, labels, logos, branded packaging, graphics, people, hands or watermark.

## Cocoa Beans

Show whole fermented and dried cocoa beans as the unmistakable hero material, loosely gathered in a restrained metal sampling scoop with a few beans scattered naturally beside it and a small fragment of dried cocoa pod as context. Beans should vary naturally in warm brown colour and size, with dry matte shells and believable imperfections. Do not show coffee beans, glossy confectionery, powder or finished chocolate.

## Cocoa Powder

Show fine cocoa powder in a low, simple ceramic sampling bowl, with a soft powder ridge and a very light natural dusting around it; include only a few broken cocoa nibs as restrained context. The powder must look dry, finely milled and industrial-food-grade, not like a dessert garnish or drink. Do not show beans as the main subject, liquid cocoa, packaging or finished baked goods.

## Cocoa Butter

Show food-grade cocoa butter as pale golden-ivory pressed blocks and a few irregular flakes, with a clean cut surface and subtle waxy crystalline texture. Add a minimal stainless sampling tool as secondary context. It must read as cocoa fat: firmer and more translucent than dairy butter, with no yellow dairy-butter styling, toast, knife spread, wrappers or finished chocolate.

## Cocoa Mass / Cocoa Liquor

Show unsweetened cocoa mass as dense dark-brown industrial blocks with irregular broken edges and a few coarse kibbled fragments, displaying a compact ground-cocoa texture rather than a glossy tempered finish. Use a restrained metal tray or sampling surface as context. It must not resemble a retail chocolate bar: no moulded squares, decorative snap lines, wrappers, sweetness cues or confectionery styling.
