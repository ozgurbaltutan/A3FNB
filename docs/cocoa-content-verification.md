# Cocoa content verification register

This register records the evidence and approvals required before the cocoa page is treated as publication-ready. It does not replace review of the original, current documents.

## Corporate and product claims

| Claim | Required evidence | Publication check |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Fairtrade cocoa products | Current certificate or transaction/supplier documentation showing the product and chain-of-custody model | Confirm certificate owner, scope, validity and whether supply is segregated or mass balance |
| Rainforest Alliance cocoa products | Current certificate or transaction/supplier documentation | Confirm certificate owner, scope, validity and traceability model |
| ISO 9001:2015 | Current certificate for the relevant legal entity or processing supplier | Confirm certificate number, scope, site and expiry |
| HACCP | Current plan, audit evidence or certificate covering the relevant processing site | Confirm product and site coverage |
| Halal and Kosher | Current certificates for each offered product or processing site | Use “where applicable” and confirm validity with each offer |
| Non-GMO supply | Product declaration, specification or supplier statement | Confirm product and lot coverage |
| Plantation-to-delivery traceability | Traceability procedure and sample lot documentation | Confirm the traceability model and records available to buyers |
| Pure Prime Pressed cocoa butter | Supplier specification and processing declaration | Confirm PPP designation for each offered natural or deodorised butter |
| Flexible financing and payment structures | Approved commercial policy and authorised offer terms | Confirm eligibility, destination, customer and credit-assessment conditions |

## Brand and legal review

- The phrase “cocoa as it is” is intentionally retained at the client's request. It must receive brand and legal review before publication because the same phrase is used publicly by Panamir.
- Superlative language such as “the world's most fertile plantations” is not used because no substantiation was supplied.
- Competitor websites may inform category structure and terminology but must not be treated as evidence for A3's own capabilities.

## Evergreen fact sources

- [Cocoa Life — Cocoa Growing](https://www.cocoalife.org/en/in-the-cocoa-origins/): typical beans per pod, approximate dried-bean equivalent and typical fermentation period. Page noted as updated June 2025.
- [FAO/WHO Codex — Code of Practice for Cocoa](https://www.fao.org/fao-who-codexalimentarius/sh-proxy/en/?lnk=1&url=https%253A%252F%252Fworkspace.fao.org%252Fsites%252Fcodex%252FMeetings%252FCX-735-07%252FREP13_CFe.pdf): fermentation and post-harvest handling guidance.
- The page deliberately does not state a fixed number of beans per 100 g chocolate because formulation, cocoa percentage and processing yield vary.
