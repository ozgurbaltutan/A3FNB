# Dried Fruit & Nuts content verification register

Reviewed on 15 July 2026. This register records the market-data basis and the technical references used to keep the page language accurate. Commercial specifications still need to be confirmed against the offered crop, supplier, lot and destination market.

## Product terminology and selection language

The [UNECE Dry and Dried Produce Standards](https://unece.org/trade/wp7/DDP-Standards) are the primary commercial-quality reference for the seven page products:

- DDP-11 for dried grapes supports commercial type, size, colour, treatment, moisture and defect language.
- DDP-15 for dried apricots supports natural/untreated versus preserved product, colour, moisture, sizing and class language. It also supports the customer-facing explanation that untreated product may be darker.
- DDP-08 for dates supports variety, pitted/unpitted form, size, moisture and defect language.
- DDP-14 for dried figs supports size, moisture, sugary tissue, defects, mould and fermentation language.
- DDP-09 and DDP-10 support the separate presentation of in-shell pistachios and pistachio kernels, including shell opening, kernel form, peeled status, colour, size and defects.
- DDP-01 and DDP-02 support the separate in-shell and kernel buying routes for walnuts, including diameter, kernel fraction, colour, moisture and defects.

## Food-safety language

- [Codex General Standard for Dried Fruits (CXS 360-2020)](https://www.fao.org/fao-who-codexalimentarius/codex-texts/standards/en/) supports general dried-fruit terminology.
- [Codex Codes of Practice](https://www.fao.org/fao-who-codexalimentarius/codex-texts/codes-of-practice) list CXC 3-1969 for dried-fruit hygiene, CXC 6-1972 for tree-nut hygiene, CXC 59-2005 for prevention and reduction of aflatoxin in tree nuts, and CXC 65-2008 for prevention and reduction of aflatoxin in dried figs.
- Aflatoxin, residues, microbiology, sulphur treatment, allergen declarations and destination documents are therefore expressed as product- and market-dependent review points, never as unsupported fixed pass/fail values.

## Presentation decision

The page deliberately has no shared specification matrix. Dried grapes, apricots, dates, figs, in-shell pistachios, pistachio kernels and walnuts do not share one useful commercial specification system. Technical facts are presented as buyer-facing selection points inside each product modal, with final values confirmed by the offered supplier specification and lot documentation.
