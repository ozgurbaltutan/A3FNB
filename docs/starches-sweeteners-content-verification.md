# Starches & Sweeteners content verification register

Reviewed on 15 July 2026. This register records the market-data basis, ingredient terminology and regulatory cautions used on the dedicated category page. Supplier specification, certificate of analysis, destination approval and application performance still need to be confirmed for every offer.

## Market context

The three page cards are a **2024 European industry snapshot published by Starch Europe in 2026**. They are sector-scale indicators, not live prices, A3 inventory or availability claims.

| Page metric | Verification basis | Source |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `22m` agricultural raw materials processed | Starch Europe reports about 22 million tonnes of mainly EU-grown wheat, maize and starch potatoes processed by the European starch industry, alongside smaller volumes of other crops. | [The European Starch Industry](https://starch.eu/the-european-starch-industry/) |
| `9.8m` starch and derivatives produced | The same industry overview reports about 9.8 million tonnes of starch and starch derivatives from 70 facilities in 18 EU Member States. | [The European Starch Industry](https://starch.eu/the-european-starch-industry/) |
| `48%` starch sweeteners | Starch Europe reports a 7.2-million-tonne EU consumption mix of 48% starch sweeteners, 34% native starch and 18% modified starch. | [The European Starch Industry](https://starch.eu/the-european-starch-industry/) |

## Product language and selection logic

- [Starch Europe Ingredients](https://starch.eu/ingredients/) supports the native starch, starch sweetener and ingredient-family framing used for customer navigation.
- [Starch Europe: Modified Starch](https://starch.eu/ingredient/modified-starch/) supports describing modified starches by the processing and storage stresses they are designed to withstand, while leaving the exact base crop, modification and labelling status to the offer.
- [Codex CXS 212-1999](https://www.fao.org/fao-who-codexalimentarius/sh-proxy/es/?lnk=1&url=https%253A%252F%252Fworkspace.fao.org%252Fsites%252Fcodex%252FStandards%252FCXS%2B212-1999%252FCXS_212e.pdf) was used to check glucose and dextrose terminology. The page distinguishes liquid glucose syrup from crystalline dextrose and separates monohydrate from anhydrous forms.
- [Codex CXS 163-1987](https://www.fao.org/input/download/standards/323/CXS_163e.pdf) was used to check vital wheat gluten language. The page presents it as concentrated wheat protein and keeps wheat-allergen controls visible.
- [UK FSA application RP-1130](https://data.food.gov.uk/regulated-product-applications/products-list/RP-1130) shows the allulose application under assessment. The page therefore treats allulose as destination-led and makes authorisation, permitted use and labelling prerequisites to commercial confirmation.
- [Commission Regulation (EU) 2021/1175](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2021/1175/oj/eng) informs the continued emphasis on destination and label review for polyol-containing products; the ingredient offer does not imply that every use or claim is permitted in every market.

## Editorial guardrails

The page deliberately has no shared technical specification matrix, origin map or supplier-specific numerical guarantee. The 21 product modals contain only decision-useful prompts: base/source, commercial form, typical fit, selection points and form-appropriate packing. Final assay, DE, viscosity, microbiology, moisture, particle size, allergen/GMO status and regulatory wording remain supplier-, grade-, destination- and lot-dependent.
