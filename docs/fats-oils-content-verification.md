# Fats & Oils content verification

Verified for the English category page on 15 July 2026. The page separates named oils, fractions and formulated fats; all supply statements remain subject to the offered product, supplier, route and destination.

## Technical terminology

- [Codex CXS 210-1999 — Named Vegetable Oils](https://www.fao.org/fao-who-codexalimentarius/sh-proxy/tr/?lnk=1&url=https%3A%2F%2Fworkspace.fao.org%2Fsites%2Fcodex%2FStandards%2FCXS+210-1999%2FCXS_210e.pdf) supports the named-oil identities used for sunflower, maize/corn, palm oil and palm olein. It distinguishes high-oleic sunflower oil and sets its oleic-acid reference at 75–90% of total fatty acids. It also identifies palm olein as the liquid fraction obtained through fractionation of palm oil.
- [Codex CXS 33-1981 — Olive Oils and Olive Pomace Oils](https://www.fao.org/fao-who-codexalimentarius/sh-proxy/en/?lnk=1&url=https%3A%2F%2Fworkspace.fao.org%2Fsites%2Fcodex%2FStandards%2FCXS+33-1981%2FCXS_033e.pdf) and the [International Olive Council standards and methods](https://www.internationaloliveoil.org/what-we-do/chemistry-standardisation-unit/standards-and-methods/) support the separation of virgin, refined and blended commercial identities and the use of analytical and sensory review where applicable.
- [Codex CXS 256-1999 — Fat Spreads and Blended Spreads](https://www.fao.org/fao-who-codexalimentarius/sh-proxy/fr/?lnk=1&url=https%3A%2F%2Fworkspace.fao.org%2Fsites%2Fcodex%2FStandards%2FCXS+256-1999%2FCXS_256e.pdf) supports the distinction between margarine identity, fat-spread identity and fat-content declarations.
- [Codex CXC 36-1987 — Storage and Transport of Edible Oils and Fats in Bulk](https://www.fao.org/fao-who-codexalimentarius/sh-proxy/en/?lnk=1&url=https%3A%2F%2Fworkspace.fao.org%2Fsites%2Fcodex%2FStandards%2FCXC+36-1987%2FCXC_036e.pdf) supports the route review for tank cleanliness, previous cargo, contamination prevention, heating and temperature control.

No universal technical-specification table is presented. FFA, peroxide value, colour, odour, moisture or impurities, fatty-acid identity, cloud or melting behaviour, certifications and destination controls are decision points to be confirmed for the specific offer.

## July 2026 USDA market calculations

Source: [USDA Oilseeds: World Markets and Trade, July 2026](https://apps.fas.usda.gov/psdonline/circulars/oilseeds.pdf), 2026/27 forecast tables.

- Major vegetable-oil production: 244.67 million tonnes versus 238.48 million tonnes for 2025/26. Calculation: `(244.67 / 238.48 - 1) × 100 = 2.60%`.
- Palm-oil exports: 45.66 million tonnes out of 92.00 million tonnes of major vegetable-oil exports. Calculation: `45.66 / 92.00 × 100 = 49.63%`, expressed on-page as approximately 50%.
- Sunflower-oil production: 23.59 million tonnes versus 21.12 million tonnes for 2025/26. Calculation: `(23.59 / 21.12 - 1) × 100 = 11.69%`. The corresponding 2026/27 export forecast is 15.62 million tonnes.

The market cards explicitly identify the forecast period and publication month and state that projections are context, not a live price indicator.
